Everything you need to know about Mersenne Numbers

This is a site which mentions about Mersenne Numbers and the Great Internet Mersenne Prime Search(GIMPS).

Overview

        Mersenne numbers are defined as numbers in the form of 2n-1, where n is a positive integer. This means 2 multiplied by itself n times, then subtracting one. Mersenne numbers are denoted as Mn.

        Mersenne primes are Mersenne numbers which are prime, i.e. having 1 and itself as its only positive divisors. The five smallest Mersenne primes are M2=3, M3=7, M5=31, M7=127 and M13=8191. Mersenne primes occur very rarely. As of 2018, only 50 Mersenne primes are known. The largest known Mersenne prime is M77232917, which also happens to be the largest known prime number. It is widely conjectured that there are infinitely many Mersenne primes, but this remains an open (unsolved) problem in mathematics.

        The Great Internet Mersenne Prime Search(GIMPS) is a distributed computing project which has a main aim of finding Mersenne primes. The last x known Mersenne primes have been found by GIMPS. For Mn to be prime, n must be prime, hence reducing the search space. Furthermore, most Mn for prime n have small factors which could be found easily, further cutting down the possibilities for finding Mersenne primes. The remaining Mersenne prime candidates are tested for primality (i.e. determine whether the number is prime) by either the Lucas-Lehmer test or a Fermat probable prime test.

        GIMPS also has a sub-project, which is to find small factors of Mn for prime n at most 20 million, hopefully yielding a complete prime factorisation. As of 2018, the smallest Mersenne number which is known to be composite but has no known factor other than 1 and itself(i.e. non-trivial factor) is M1277.

        GIMPS has another sub-project, which is to find factors of Fermat numbers, which are numbers in the form 22n+1, for non-negative integers n. However, this is not the main focus of this webpage because the webpage is supposed to talk about Mersenne numbers instead of Fermat numbers.
